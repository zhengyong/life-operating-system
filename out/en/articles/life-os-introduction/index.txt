b:T5bf,<p>Life is not a sprint. It is a long-term systems engineering project.</p>
<p>When we understand life as a system, we naturally pay attention to inputs, structure, feedback, constraints, and long-term outputs. What a person needs to build is not only isolated capability, but an operating system that can keep improving.</p>
<h2>Why Life OS Matters</h2>
<p>Modern life is increasingly complex. Education, career, investing, technology, family, and social responsibility influence one another. A decision in one domain may reshape the result in another.</p>
<p>Life OS tries to answer three questions:</p>
<ol>
<li>What values are worth pursuing over the long term?</li>
<li>How can first-principles thinking reveal the structure beneath a problem?</li>
<li>How can models, methods, and tools become stable action?</li>
</ol>
<h2>Models, Methods, and Tools</h2>
<p>Models help us understand reality. Methods help us decompose problems. Tools improve execution. These three layers should not be separated.</p>
<p>Models without methods often stay conceptual. Methods without models can become mechanical routines. Tools without values and judgment may amplify the wrong direction.</p>
<h2>Long-Term Compounding</h2>
<p>The goal of Life OS is to help ordinary people compound over time: cognitively, professionally, relationally, financially, and civilizationally.</p>
<p>This system will continue to expand through articles, books, courses, AI tools, and communities.</p>
