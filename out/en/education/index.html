Learning Framework

Learning Framework

Map Life OS onto learning so every cycle has input, understanding, choice, action, feedback, and upgrade.

1

Perception

Observe reality, interest, problems, and environment; collect high-quality input.

2

Cognition

Build concepts, models, and causal understanding; turn information into structure.

3

Decision

Choose goals, paths, and resources; clarify priorities and constraints.

4

Execution

Act through reading, practice, projects, expression, and collaboration.

5

Feedback

Calibrate understanding and method through results, work, discussion, and tests.

6

Evolution

Distill models, adjust the system, and convert one learning cycle into long-term capability.

Learning Growth Path

From input to evolution, each learning cycle becomes better judgment, action, and self-management for the next one.

InputOutputUpgrade

Education Stages

Education Stages

Each age has a different emphasis, but the core goal is consistent: protect curiosity, train capability, and build self-direction.

0

0-6

Kindergarten

0-6

Protect security, curiosity, and language expression while forming basic habits.

Key Abilities

  • Sensory exploration
  • Language expression
  • Emotion recognition

Recommended Methods

  • Shared reading
  • Nature observation
  • Play-based learning

Recommended Books

  • Picture books, nature encyclopedias, children poetry

Practice Case

  • Observe one plant for a week, then draw, ask questions, and explain changes.
6

6-12

Primary School

6-12

Build reading, math, expression, and habit systems; begin forming learning confidence.

Key Abilities

  • Foundational reading
  • Mathematical intuition
  • Clear expression

Recommended Methods

  • Reading journal
  • Mistake review
  • Small project demos

Recommended Books

  • Early chapter books, science readers, history stories

Practice Case

  • Research where water comes from at home and produce a hand-drawn flow chart.
12

12-15

Middle School

12-15

Move from passive to active learning while training logic, review, and resilience.

Key Abilities

  • Abstract understanding
  • Problem decomposition
  • Self-review

Recommended Methods

  • Concept cards
  • Feynman explanation
  • Periodic review

Recommended Books

  • General introductions, history of science, biographies

Practice Case

  • Break one exam failure into knowledge, method, time, and mindset causes.
15

15-18

High School

15-18

Build a stable system across academic depth, goal choice, and pressure management.

Key Abilities

  • Systematic learning
  • Long-term planning
  • Pressure management

Recommended Methods

  • Knowledge maps
  • Focused breakthroughs
  • Goal-action sheets

Recommended Books

  • Subject classics, writing practice, mental models

Practice Case

  • Use quarterly goals to improve one weak subject from diagnosis to practice and feedback.
18+

18+

University

18+

Form expertise, interdisciplinary perspective, and a real-world portfolio.

Key Abilities

  • Professional depth
  • Interdisciplinary connection
  • Portfolio building

Recommended Methods

  • Research-based learning
  • Internships and projects
  • Public writing

Recommended Books

  • Textbooks, papers, business and technology cases

Practice Case

  • Run an eight-week project around a real problem and ship a paper, codebase, or prototype.
